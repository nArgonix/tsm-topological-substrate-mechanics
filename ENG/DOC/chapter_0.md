# Topological Substrate Mechanics (TSM)

> *Presented here is a proposal for the unification of General Relativity and the Standard Model. Grounded in data accumulated through decades of observations and experimental research, TSM (Topological Substrate Mechanics) merges these two pillars of modern physics into a single, coherent framework. The formulation of this mathematical formalism would not have been possible without building upon the groundbreaking work of numerous outstanding theoretical physicists and mathematicians. TSM synthesizes their key achievements, reshaping the existing, rigorous conceptual framework into a unified language for describing reality that transcends current divisions.*

## Chapter 0: Axioms of the 0-Matrix, or the mechanical "engine" of reality

Before proceeding to the formal mathematical apparatus, we must establish the foundation upon which the entire visible Universe rests. For decades, theoretical physics has been characterized by the introduction of abstract equations while neglecting the most fundamental question: what is the world mechanically built of? This chapter strips reality of its mathematical fog and returns to the foundations of kinematics. Its primary objective is to reset the researcher's intuition and liberate the inferential process from the dogmas ingrained in modern physics, which conflate mathematical abstraction with physical reality.

### 0.1. Rejection of the non-physical void

One of the greatest challenges in modern physics is explaining the nature of the vacuum. Intuition suggests that a wave is always a disturbance of a medium: a stone thrown into water creates propagating ripples, and sound requires air to reach our ears. Classical physics has long accepted this as self-evident; however, when transitioning to light or radio waves, we suddenly accept the notion that they transmit energy through absolute nothingness. This is a logical inconsistency that we can no longer ignore.

We reject this dogma by introducing the first axiom: space is not empty. It is filled with fundamental elements of matter—0-particles. Their dimensions are sub-Planckian, rendering them completely imperceptible as "objects" to our current technologies, yet they constitute a uniform, dense substrate of reality.

These particles possess no charges or complex internal structure. As the simplest, indivisible units of matter, they are characterized by an identical, elementary mass and—critically—a **strictly defined, non-zero intrinsic volume ($V_0 > 0$)**. We thus exclude from the theory the abstract concept of a mathematical "material point" with zero dimension. In TSM, every constituent element of space possesses physical dimensions. In this model, every energy state of the substrate reduces to pure kinematics—momentum resulting from the incessant collisions of the spherical representations of these particles. Everything we observe as physical phenomena is exclusively a macroscopic echo of these microscopic, constant collisions.

### 0.2. Elasticity from pure kinematics

Traditional physics perceives the elasticity of materials through the prism of "invisible springs"—forces that connect particles and provide resistance when attempting to change their relative positions. In our model, we reject this description because it requires the assumption of forces within a structure prior to explaining the very nature of force. We introduce no additional force fields or attractive interactions. Instead, we derive all elasticity solely from the dynamics of 0-particles and their momentum.

Imagine a 0-particle trapped in a closed volume (an interaction sphere). In a state of equilibrium, the particle oscillates, striking the boundaries of its sphere at a specific frequency. What happens when an external force begins to compress this space? The mean free path of the particle is shortened. While maintaining constant momentum, the return time between collisions decreases drastically, and the frequency of impacts against the chamber walls increases exponentially.

For a macroscopic observer, this rapid increase in collision frequency is equivalent to an increase in pressure. This creates an elastic structure that resists compression—not due to "hidden forces," but due to pure geometry and kinematics. This law forms the foundation of our theory: elasticity is here an emergent property of collision density. The more we reduce the space, the more powerful the resistance, which we perceive on a macro scale as the ideal elasticity of the medium. However, this mechanism possesses a hard, geometric limit: compression cannot proceed infinitely, as it encounters an impassable threshold of maximum random packing ($\phi_{\text{max}}$), at which 0-particles contact one another at their physical boundaries.

### 0.3. 4D Pressure Vessel: Finiteness as a source of stress

If the Universe were infinite, the momentum of 0-particles would dissipate irrevocably, and the collisions would cease. For reality to exist as a stable structure, it must satisfy the condition of finiteness.

Our 4-dimensional space functions in this model like a giant, closed container. Because 0-particles have nowhere to escape, they are forced into continuous collisions. This permanent, kinematic pressure ensures that space is never relaxed—it remains in a state of perpetual stress, enabling it to propagate vibrations instantaneously. In this view, the Universe is not filled with energy; it *is* that energy trapped in a finite geometry.

### 0.4. Relaxation State: Autoregulation of 0-Particles

Each 0-particle strives for a state of relaxation—a point where the pressure of impacts from one side perfectly balances the pressure from neighboring 0-particles. On a micro scale, this means that a local concentration of 0-particles immediately triggers a sharp increase in their kinetic activity, which the system interprets as "excess pressure".

This phenomenon is analogous to pressure equalization in a gas tank: particles in a region of higher collision density inevitably transfer their momentum to areas of lower concentration, striving to unify pressure throughout the container. The system does not leave excess energy in one place—it immediately dissipates it through a cascade of collisions with neighbors. This mechanism ensures that no local deformation can remain permanent in a low-energy regime. If any factor disturbs this arrangement, a pressure wave will propagate throughout the entire system until all return to a state of equilibrium. We call this drive toward constant stress equalization the "relaxation" of the 0-Matrix.

### 0.5. Birth of the Membrane (3D Self-Organization)

How did our ordered world emerge from 4-dimensional chaos? The answer is provided by wave mechanics and the physics of continuous media. When we pour fine sand onto a vibrating membrane, the grains do not disperse chaotically but form precise geometric patterns—so-called Chladni figures. Similarly, 0-particles, under the influence of permanent pressure waves, are forced to self-organize into a giant, coherent network.

Spatial geometry dictates that a stable phase boundary in a 4-dimensional medium possesses exactly 3 dimensions. Our Universe is precisely such an elastic, 3-dimensional Membrane suspended in the depth of the 4th dimension. Everything we observe is vibrations propagating across this giant, oscillating "membrane." When this structure undergoes a local, non-linear distortion (as a result of exceeding the substrate's plasticity threshold), a permanent topological braid—a physical particle—is formed within it.

Because this distortion occupies volume in the fourth dimension as well, it causes a permanent bulge in our Membrane. This local curvature, resulting from the presence of the knot, creates a stress gradient in the surrounding structure. It is this geometric deformation of the Membrane toward the 4th dimension, induced by the pressure of the surrounding matrix, that we perceive macroscopically as gravity.

### 0.6. Axiom of Physical Finiteness: Categorical exclusion of singularities and "black holes"

At this point, we must formulate a fundamental logical postulate that represents the deepest demarcation line between TSM and the 20th-century legacy of General Relativity (GR) and standard quantum mechanics.

**The TSM theory introduces the Axiom of Physical Finiteness: in the real world, infinities do not exist.** Mathematical concepts in which any physical parameter—such as density, pressure, temperature, or spatial curvature—approaches infinity ($\infty$) are merely artifacts of imperfect, continuum-like mathematical models devoid of a mechanistic basis.

A consequence of the non-zero intrinsic volume of 0-particles ($V_0 > 0$) and the existence of a geometric limit to their packing ($\phi_{\text{max}}$) is the categorical prohibition against any region of space collapsing into a dimensionless point. Because 0-particles possess hard kinetic cores, no force in the Universe—including the most extreme gravitational pressure—is capable of compressing matter below its structural geometric limit.

For this reason, the TSM model definitively and unambiguously excludes the existence of **gravitational singularities**, thereby overturning the concept of "black holes" in their classical, relativistic sense (as objects of infinite density hidden behind an event horizon). Astronomical objects that modern astrophysics observes in the centers of galaxies and incorrectly interprets as black holes are, in reality, physical, volumetric structures of finite, extreme density. **In the TSM architecture, these objects are defined as degenerate, macroscopic matrix braids—plasmoids.** A plasmoid is an ultra-efficient, relativistic mechanical engine, which is a natural consequence of the 0-particle packing limit. Its exact hydrodynamic, electromagnetic, and topological nature will be described in detail in subsequent chapters of this work, but for now, the reader must accept this physical substitute and reject the dogma of infinite gravitational collapse to correctly analyze substrate mechanics.

### 0.7. Creation of the first distortions: Alternative to the Big Bang

In our model, we must draw a clear boundary of cognition. The question of what gave 0-particles their initial momentum and what caused the first "distortions" in the 0-Matrix remains an open issue. Inquiry into the causes of the system's initial state lies beyond the strict scope of this theory; it is a sphere of pure philosophical speculation, not verifiable mechanics.

We can, however, based on the Axiom of Physical Finiteness, formulate a rigorous alternative to the classical "Big Bang" concept. Since the initial singularity phase is geometrically impossible due to the incompressibility of the 0-particles themselves, the TSM model rejects a single, non-physical act of creation of everything from a point of zero volume.

Instead, the model permits a scenario that is much more rational and consistent with the physics of continuous media. Within our 4-dimensional container, countless local and large-scale pressure fluctuations have always occurred and continue to occur. A powerful, cumulative wave of kinetic energy, striking the already-formed, 3-dimensional Membrane, forces its violent, local, non-linear "folding." It is in this way, through the local crossing of the substrate's plasticity thresholds and the mass generation of permanent topological braids, that the first clusters of matter in the Universe were formed and can continuously be formed.
